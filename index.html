A Further Look Into Air Pollution Map
Air pollution has become an issue of global concern, affecting both our environment and the health of the world's population. The alteration of the atmosphere by harmful chemicals has been linked to various respiratory and cardiovascular conditions. This dataset aims to provide insights into air quality metrics across various cities and countries.
The chart offers a clear representation of the Air Quality Index (AQI) values for various countries. The width of each segment signifies the magnitude of the AQI value for that country. Countries with larger segments, like the dominant green one, have higher AQI values, suggesting more air pollution.It paints a comprehensive picture of global air pollution. Countries with significant segments and darker shades are areas of concern, indicating higher levels of pollution.
Ozone Air Quality Index (AQI) Values by City
Both China and India, two of the world's most populated and rapidly industrializing nations, showcase elevated Ozone AQI values, signaling higher concentrations of ground-level ozone, a primary component in urban smog.High ozone levels can have dire health implications, exacerbating respiratory issues and potentially increasing the incidence of cardiovascular problems.In contrast, countries like Australia and France depict significantly lower Ozone AQI values. These nations, while industrialized, have stringent environmental regulations and policies in place to mitigate air pollution.
